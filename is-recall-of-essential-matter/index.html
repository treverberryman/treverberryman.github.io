Recall of Essential Matter

Gen Ed:

  • ENGL 2300: Rhetorical Analysis
    • I completed this course in the in the Spring of 2013.
    • Statement:
      • I learned about how to use cultural and rhetorical analysis as a lens to interpret discourse (text, visual, audio). We also covered the rhetoric triangle which is composed of Logos, Pathos, and Ethos. My final project was to rhetorically analyze the video game “Bioshock.”

Digital Media Technology Minor (DMTEC):

  • MUS 1751: Music Appreciation
    • I completed this course in the Spring of 2012.
    • Statement:
      • In this course I learned about the techniques of how to appreciate music first and foremost. We covered multiple music periods such as, such as the Baroque period. Each section we covered composers, the theory behind each composer and the instruments unique to that period.
  • CSC 1350: Computer Science I for Majors
    • I completed this course in the Fall of 2012.
    • Statement:
    • I learned the fundamentals of algorithm development, foundational programming design and structured programming using the OOP language, Java.
  • CSC 1351: Computer Science II for Majors
    • I completed this course in the Spring of 2013.
    • Statement:
      • In this course I developed solutions to problems using more OO approaches and the course emphasized the concepts of recursion, dynamic memory, data structures (lists, stacks, queues, trees) and exception handling.
  • CSC 3380: Object Oriented Design
    • I completed this course in the Fall of 2016.
    • Statement:
    • This course covers advanced object oriented software development; with an emphasis on the use of the Unified Modeling Language (UML) as a design tool.
  • CSC 4263: Video Game Design
    • I completed this course in the Spring of 2016.
    • Statement:
    • This course is an introduction to the theory and practice of video game design and programming. Video game programming attempts to combine, in real time, concepts in: computer graphics, human computer interaction, networking, artificial intelligence, computer aided instruction, computer architecture, and databases. Topics we covered include: Gameplay design, software architecture, an intro to 3D computer graphics, game artificial intelligence, multiplayer design, game physics, the Unity3D engine, art asset management, hardware architecture of contemporary game platforms, human physical / perceptual limits with stereoscopic computer graphics, intro to post-processing effects, the taxonomy of video games, and video game history.
  • CSC 4356: Interactive Computer Graphics
    • I completed this course in the Fall of 2015
    • Statement:
    • This course provided an introduction to 3D computer graphics, which emphasized real-time graphics programming using both WebGL and JavaScript programming languages. Topics included an introduction to JavaScript, the OpenGL ES shading language, the mathematics of 3D graphics, transformation, texturing, lighting, scene management, and interaction. Topics in software engineering, including project management and source code control, were also covered. I also gained experience in developing interactive 3D content running across Windows, Linux, and Mac OS X in a web browser on my own hardware.
  • MC 2035: Digital Brands
    • I completed this course in the Spring of 2015.
    •  Statement:
    • This course explored how digital, social and mobile media change existing media systems and persuasive practices, and how they are used effectively to develop personal and corporate brands. I also learned how to effectively keep up with the rapidly changing environment in regards to digital personal brands and digital business brands. Some challenges we worked through include:
      • Learning and applying theories of persuasion to social media to effectively brand myself using digital media,
      • learning the history, development and capabilities of digital, social and mobile media,
      • examining societal implications of digital media on privacy, identity, attention, authenticity and credibility,
      • picking apart the challenges digital presents to the advertising, public relations, political communications and journalism industries
      • and learning how to effectively and appropriately respond to audiences via social media.
  • EE 4859: Digital Media Technology Capstone
    • I take this course in Spring 2017.

ISDS (IT-Management) Minor:

  • ISDS 1102: Introduction to Management Information Systems for Business Majors
    • I completed this course in the Spring of 2015.
    • Statement:
    • This course was a general introduction to the IT Management minor (ISDS). We learned how to use most of the programs in Microsoft Office as well as how to operate the Windows 8 operating system.
  • ISDS 3100: Management of Information Resources
    • I completed this course in the Fall of 2015.
    • Statement:
    • This course had two objectives. The first objective was to help me prepare myself for getting an internship or job. The second objective was for me to learn about some of the issues, processes, techniques and best practices associated with organizational software analysis and design. Specifically, I focused on the first two phases of the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) which are planning and analysis.
  • ISDS 3107: Beginning Programming
    • I completed this course in the Fall of 2015.
    • Statement:
    • In this course I went over the fundamentals of programming, program design, application development interfaces, debugging, testing and implementation. C# was the language of choice.
  • ISDS 4113: Information Technology Project Management
    • I completed this course in the Spring of 2016.
    • Statement:
    • This course covered concepts and skills for effective project management, including project management tools/techniques and the socio-cultural aspects of the project management process. Technical topics included project selection, project scope statements, work breakdown structure (WBS), estimation, scheduling of resources, progress evaluation, managing risk, Agile project management, and project audit/closure. Socio-cultural topics included team development, team processes (communication, conflict resolution, negotiation), personality types in teams, team leadership, stakeholder management, and ethical issues of projects. Assignments included application of project planning and technical skills and analysis of project cases. Concepts and skills were taught through a combination of readings, lectures, discussions, and assignments. MS Project software was used for some of the assignments.
  • ISDS 4117: Management of E-commerce and Internet Information Systems
    • I completed this course in the Spring of 2016.
    • Statement:
    • The course introduced critical issues in the management of e-commerce and internet systems. It was also very hands-on. I participated in several team projects and presented the results to executives from the sponsoring companies at the end of the semester. I gained experience analyzing and interpreting large sets of real data provided by the instructor.
  • ISDS 3110: Database Processing for Management
  • I completed this course in the Fall of 2016.
    • Statement:
    • This course provides a basic understanding of information management in organizations and specifically the design and use of database systems. Topics include data modeling, relational databases, and structured query language (SQL).

International Studies Minor:

  • ANTH 1003: Introduction into Social and Cultural Anthropology
    • I completed this course in the Fall of 2015.
    • Statement:
    • Using readings, films, and examples from around the world, this course provided a broad introduction, including both classic anthropological topics such as kinship, religion, and exchange, as well as current developments like environmental and medical anthropology. Anthropology “makes the strange familiar and the familiar strange.”
  • HIST 1007: World History Since 1500
    • I completed this course in the Spring of 2015.
    • Statement:
    • Interactions among Asian, Middle Eastern, African, European and American cultures in the modern era. As the course title suggests we covered history since 1500 BCE.
  • POLI 2057: Introduction to International Politics
    • I completed this course in the Spring of 2015.
    • Statement:
    • The course granted me a broad-based exposure, across a variety of conceptual and empirical issues, to the current international system. The issues were contextualized within both normative and empirical frameworks which equipped me with a sophisticated and multi-dimensional analytical toolbox for IR.
  • SPAN 2102: Intermediate Spanish
    • I completed this course in the Spring of 2015.
    • Statement:
    • The primary goal of SPAN 2102 was to review previously learned Spanish in my prior experience and to help acquire new communicative skills in Spanish while developing an awareness and appreciation of Hispanic/Latino cultures.
  • SOCL 4551: Global Society
    • I completed this course in the in the Spring of 2016.
    • Statement:
    • In this course I was taught to “think globally, act locally.” This course primarily concentrated on the global part of this adage; and I was encouraged to think about the local implications. Specifically, the course encouraged me to think critically about globalization, in all its many forms and definitions, and how it impacts countries around the world and also families and communities in the United States and Louisiana.
  • POLI 4064: Comparative Politics in Developing Areas
    • This course is in progress.
    • Statement:
    • The course is designed to analyze the forces and trends characteristic of politics in developing countries. We will use all examples of all the major regions in the Third World (Asia, Africa, Latin America, and the Middle East) in order to understand the pervasive nature of the political crisis that has enveloped the developing world.
  • INTL 2000: Contemporary Global Issues
    • I completed this course in the in the Spring of 2012.
    • Statement:
    • This course examined global issues and the problems that we are currently facing as a global society. There was an emphasis on the relationship between society and technology. Some of the issues addressed were: international property and intellectual rights, space exploration, scientific commons, communication, biotechnology, environment, population and “outside context problems.” These are issues which must be addressed in global context, and from an interdisciplinary perspective because they do not stop at national border, nor do they involve only one particular aspect of human interaction. Throughout this course, we focused on the global institutions, policies, norms and cultural elements that guide our interactions.
  • WGS 2900: Gender, Race, and Nation
    • I completed this course in the in the Fall of 2015.
    • Statement:
    • One of the objectives of women’s studies is to uncover the experiences of women. Since women make up 50% of the population, it is impossible to speak about women as singular group. There is no such thing as a woman’s experience, and what it means to be a woman or a man varies across time, place, and space. The purpose of this course was to explore differing gendered experiences across racial, ethnic, cultural, and class divides. We began the course with a consideration of the social construction of the categories gender, race, and nation. We then moved on to explore how these differences shape individuals’ experiences across a wide array of issues including health, militarism, labor, economics, politics, and migration.

Sociology Minor:

  • SOCL 2001: Introductory Sociology
    • I completed this course in the in the Fall of 2015.
    • Statement:
    • This course provided me with a broad overview of the major subject areas and principles of sociology, the scientific study of human society and social behavior. This course will covered some of the basic theoretical perspectives and research methods sociologists use to examine the social world. In addition, this course covered topics such as: relationships between individuals and groups; socialization; deviance and crime; social class inequality; race/ethnicity; gender; and social institutions such as the family, religion, and the educational system. The goal of this course was to develop my sociological imagination—the ability to think critically about the linkages amongst individual experiences, history, and the workings of society.
  • SOCL 3501: Sociology of Deviance
    • This course is in progress.
    • Statement:
    • Sociological theories of deviant behavior; supporting research on mental illness, crime, sexual deviance, drug abuse and suicide.
  • SOCL 3601: Social Interaction
    • I completed this course in the in the Spring of 2016.
    • Statement:
    • This course was about human behavior as social interaction. I learned a lot about social development, social interaction theories and social analysis applications.
