Integrative Learning

      To begin, the minors in my BIS degree are Digital Media Tech, IT Management (ISDS), and International Studies. My future endeavor is to produce change through video games. I first realized that I wanted to make Serious Games when I was researching ways on “how to change the world.” I saw a specific need that could be filled in the video game community by being a game tester for three years with EA at LSU. The way I plan to implement my games will be to run analytics based on how the player is responding to the game then make insights based on that data. With those insights, I will be able to fill the needs where they need to be met for whichever social problem I am tackling. I knew that my career was going to be in developing games, but I didn’t realize I wanted something more. 

      The digital media tech minor provides me with a means of expression through the video games I would make. I understand that I need to know what it takes to run or participate in a gaming business and the ISDS minor provided that crucial information. The international studies minor integrates with my other minors because it influences my perspective on the social challenges I will tackle. When I took classes for that minor I was made aware of so many different social problems that do not exist in the United States.

      After taking my first sociology course I realized that I could use this social science to gather quantitative and qualitative social data coming from the received input in my games. I learned about the various ways of how to collect sociological data, theories, and applications which integrated into my toolset for following my passion. All of these connections in and outside academia led me to the insight of making games for social change as a career.
