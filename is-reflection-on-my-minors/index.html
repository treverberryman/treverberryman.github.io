Reflection on My Minors

Digital Media tech is my most important minor out of the four because it is directly connected to game development. I think that in the beginning, before I even had the DMTEC minor, Music Appreciation helped me understand that the arts are important to the world and that politics and technology are heavily influenced by the elegance that the arts brings. I would say that Music Appreciation helped me appreciate more than just music. If I am being honest I think it helped me appreciate video games as an art form which in turn helped me get to where I am
today.

My very first programming class at LSU was CSC 1350, Computer Science I for Majors. The class was arduous and difficult. Even though the professor I had wasn’t the greatest, I still gained the importance of working with others on programming assignments and projects together. In that beginning class I conceived the CSC Guild which is a Facebook group that exists today. It serves as away to connect all LSU Computing students in one Facebook group. It’s been extremely successful in organizing study groups and solving difficult problems. I think also that I was able to get a good start on building my foundational programming skills, but it was really Computer Science II for Majors that cemented my foundation as a programmer in and out of school.

Computer Science II for Majors is in my top 10 so far of best and most rewarding classes at LSU. The main reason it was so much of a success is because the professor, Dr. Brandt, is a great teacher. Most of my programming chops and foundation comes from his class. I use a lot of what I learned there in my professional life and and the courses I took afterwards / now. He pushed us to think critically in live coding examples in class as well which helped develop my skills as a programmer.

Object Oriented Design, which I’m taking now seems to be heavily influenced by ISDS 4113 (IT Project Management). The class is using a loosely based Agile framework to complete the final project, but since I have more experience with Agile from another course, our group has a leg up or two. Unfortunately, this course has forced me to teach myself in many regards since our professor isn’t the best. When I took CS 2 for Majors I learned about UML diagrams and in this course we’re taking the next steps to add complexity to those diagrams.

Video Game Design was the cementing course that told me I need to be a game development programmer. It pushed me to make my own games outside of class using what I learned in the course. Game design gave me the same quality of foundation that CS 2 for Majors did. I think I probably would not have learned as much as a I did if I tried learning game development on my own. It’s really spring boarded me onto the right path. I learned a ton of skills that are very marketable in the game industry to employers. I know more know what it takes to make games. All my programming course prior helped me do well in Game Design.

Interactive Computer Graphics is also in my top 10 courses rewarding classes. I now use a lot of the tools from this course outside the classroom. I also learned three new programming languages from this course alone which is phenomenal. I feel that this course was a semi-primer to video game design because it covered a fair number of the same topics. I also got a lot more experience doing code debugging. We used the chrome debugger to debug JavaScript and we also debugged WebGL and GLSL code.

Digital Brands wasn’t like any of the other courses I’ve taken in the DMTEC minor or even at LSU. This course essentially helped craft my own digital “brand”, which is essentially my identity online. This has helped me understand what it takes to sell myself and make myself marketable in the industry. I learned a lot of ways to analyze social data which actually integrates well with what I want to do in the future in terms of civil-sociological research and application.

IT Management is a support minor that for me personally integrates into my DMTEC minor as an IT backbone.

Intro to ISDS wasn’t really a hard course at all. Personally, I already had a strong technical background so I didn’t see anything new really except for how to use Windows 8 more effectively.

ISDS 3100, Management of Information Resources, was one of the more interesting and rewarding classes I’ve taken in the IT Management minor. This integrated nicely with Digital Brands because they both helped me be more marketable and to prepare myself for my profession. We learned about the Software Development Life Cycle which pervades all of the software projects I’ve done. After this class, I understood the SDLC better which increased my ability to complete projects in and outside the classroom faster and more efficiently.

Beginning programming really helped me learn CSharp because it was selected language for all programming in the course. Luckily enough, the Unity3D game engine heavily uses Sharp which really helped me learn Unity much faster. This course also reinvigorated my love of programming. I hadn’t taken any programming classes until this one since I dropped out of Computer Science the year before.

IT Project Management, as mentioned before was really useful in and out of class. I learned a ton about Agile frameworks which are used heavily in the game industry. I received hands on experience using Agile which gave me a strong foundation for project management.

E-commerce was definitely an interesting course. I would say it was rewarding for sure. This course was also really hands on just like Project Management. I think this course drew a lot from what I learned in Digital Brands as well in the sense that we covered what it takes to be marketable on the web and the tools you would need to use to get there.

Database Management has been really rewarding so far because we’re learning about the Structure Query Language (SQL). This will be really useful down the line when I get into working with databases on a professional level.

International studies is what gives me the perspective to stay humble understand the worlds problems and its intricacies.

I actually had to take Intro to Social and Cultural Anthropology twice, and the second time is when it was way more rewarding. This is only course that I’ve taken at LSU that has given me a foundation in Anthropology. Since it focused in the social-cultural area of anthropology it gave me more perspective in my sociology courses.

I’ve taken an AP world history course in high school before and I enjoyed that course. World History since 1500 was also very interesting. The professor focused a good amount on hispanic-latin history since that’s what his field pertains to in his doctorate. I already had a couple Spanish courses prior to his course and those helped me understand what I learned in this course.

Intro to International Politics was actually my second political science class and it helped give me more foundation for most all of my international studies classes. The prior class to this that helped build what I know was Contemporary Global Issues.

Intermediate Spanish was also really rewarding. This course helped me practice my Spanish language skills on a daily basis. Personally, I would like to be fluent in Spanish at some point in my life and any practice helps.

Global Society was a very hands on course for me. The course seemed very interdisciplinary because it’s labeled a sociology course, but it also incorporates political science and theory. Intro to International Politics helped out a lot with this course.

Comparative Politics in Developing Areas is a course I’m taking this semester and I think it builds on a lot of the topics we covered in my Intro to International Politics. This course is helping me become more aware of what issues are going on in the Third World and those that could be solved with what my project will cover.

Contemporary Global Issues was the first class that turned me onto International Studies. Without it I wouldn’t have this minor. This course gave me the inspiration to tackled “contemporary global issues.”

The one WGS class I’ve taken so far was Gender, Race, and Nation. This course makes my top 10 most rewarding classes at LSU. I ended up excelling in this course as well. This WGS class has given me new insights about new points of view I didn’t have before and it influenced what I will do in the future in terms of solving problems. I will be taking another course, Sociology of Gender, in the Spring that will build on this.

Sociology is what gives me the tools to study groups of people in terms of what I’ll be doing in years to come. Sociology was added after I already had enough matching credits from international studies.

Intro to Sociology was my first sociology course at LSU It was pretty great as well. We covered many sociological theories and research methods. This course gave me inspiration to help find a way to solve social problems around the world. I never had the tools to understand how to remedy the problems. This class provided a good foundation for all my other sociology classes.

Social Interaction was an enlightening course. It provided a lot of insight into how I viewed my own social interaction and how I could improve it through collegiate study. This course compounded with Intro to Sociology.

Sociology of Deviance has given me even more tools to study sociological issues around the world. As stated we look into the sociology of deviance.
